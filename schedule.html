Comparative Shape Analysis • Spring 2026

Default cadence: readings before class; AMES; deliverable due Friday 11:59pm ET (unless noted).

Key Spring 2026 calendar notes (BC)

  • Classes begin: Mon Jan 12, 2026
  • MLK Day (no classes): Mon Jan 19, 2026
  • Spring recess (no classes): Mon–Fri Mar 2–6, 2026
  • Easter recess (no classes): Thu–Mon Apr 2–6, 2026 (classes resume Apr 6 at 4pm)
  • Patriot’s Day (no classes): Mon Apr 20, 2026
  • Spring Half-Term 2 ends: Thu Apr 30, 2026
  • Study days / exam period: see the Registrar’s final exam schedule for your section.

(These items are intentionally high-level—your individual meeting pattern determines which specific “no class” dates affect you.)

Phase I: Core toolkit (Weeks 1–8)

Week 1 (Jan 12): What is “shape,” and how do we measure it reproducibly?

Topics - Shape vs size; biological meaning of “shape” - Reproducible workflows (R projects, here(), R Markdown, Git/GitHub) - First biological case study: ecomorphology in fishes (or sharks)

Deliverable: AME 1 — repo setup + 1 figure + short interpretation

Week 2 (Jan 19): Univariate characters (and why they still matter)

MLK Day: if your section meets on Monday, this is an async lab week.

Topics - Trait definition, scaling, ratios, allometry - Measurement error and repeatability - Univariate hypothesis testing designs

Deliverable: AME 2 — univariate model + diagnostic + decision log

Week 3 (Jan 26): Landmarks, Procrustes, and the geometry of form

Topics - Landmarks/semilandmarks; sliding - GPA, centroid size, tangent space - Deformation visualization (TPS grids, vectors)

Deliverable: AME 3: PCA + deformation figure + biological claim

Week 4 (Feb 2): Outlines + Fourier descriptors

Topics - When landmarks fail: outlines and complex boundaries - Elliptic Fourier descriptors; reconstruction + harmonics - Biological examples: teeth, fins, leaves, shells

Deliverable: AME 4 — outline PCA + one classifier/clustering analysis

Week 5 (Feb 9): From ordination to inference (multivariate thinking)

Topics - PCA as a tool (and the limits of PC fishing) - Effect sizes, resampling, cross-validation ideas - Trajectories and modularity (conceptual + simple workflows)

Deliverable: AME 5 — group comparison + uncertainty summary

Week 6 (Feb 16): Comparative methods I (univariate PCM, properly)

Topics - Phylogenetic signal (conceptual) - PGLS, phylogenetic ANOVA; model diagnostics - Trait–environment association example

Deliverable: AME 6 — PGLS + “what would change my mind?” paragraph

Week 7 (Feb 23): Comparative methods II (multivariate PCM + shape)

Topics - mvPGLS, phylogenetic MANOVA, distance-based alternatives - High-dimensional shape: RRPP logic; regularization concept - Tree-linked visualization (evolutionary patterns)

Deliverable: AME 7 — multivariate PCM test + tree visualization

Week 8 (Mar 9): Evolutionary models of shape (BM, OU, rates, disparity)

Spring recess is Mar 2–6, so this week lands immediately after recess.

Topics - BM vs OU intuition for shape data - Rate heterogeneity + regime shifts (claims you can/can’t make) - Disparity through time; clade comparisons

Deliverable: AME 8 — model comparison figure + limitations section

Phase II: Independent project block (Weeks 9–13)

Your project must include: 1) a biological question,
2) shape/trait data, and
3) at least one explicit comparative/evolutionary component.

Week 9 (Mar 16): Project scoping + proposal clinic

In class - Idea triage: question → data → methods → feasible scope - Repo hygiene for research projects

Deliverable: Proposal (1–2 pages) + project repo skeleton

Week 10 (Mar 23): Data audit + measurement error / sampling plan

In class - Data provenance, cleaning, missingness checks - Measurement error plan (repeat digitizing, bootstrap, sensitivity)

Deliverable: Data audit notebook + updated README (data provenance)

Week 11 (Mar 30): Analysis plan + prereg-style decision log

In class - What’s confirmatory vs exploratory? - Mapping hypotheses to models + plots

Deliverable: Analysis plan + decision log + figure mockups

Week 12 (Apr 6): Midpoint check-in (one model, one figure, one claim)

Easter recess is Apr 2–6; if your section meets early in the week, we’ll adjust check-ins.

Deliverable: Midpoint release (tagged) + short reflection

Week 13 (Apr 13–Apr 27): Writing, polishing, and presenting

Patriot’s Day is Apr 20 (no classes) and may affect meeting patterns.

Deliverables (end of window) - Final report (reproducible HTML + PDF) - 10–12 minute presentation - v1.0 tagged release + README + CITATION

Suggested due-date map (if you want a single list)

  • AMEs 1–8: Fridays of Weeks 1–8
  • Project proposal: Fri Mar 20, 2026
  • Data audit: Fri Mar 27, 2026
  • Analysis plan: Fri Apr 3, 2026 (shift if your section is impacted by Easter recess)
  • Midpoint check-in: Fri Apr 10, 2026
  • Final materials: Fri May 1, 2026 (or your scheduled final/presentation slot)
