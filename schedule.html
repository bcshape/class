Schedule

Phase I: The Ins and Outs of Shape

Week 1 (Jan 12): What is “shape”?

Topics

  • Shape vs size; biological meaning of “shape”
  • Data types and definitions: univariate, multivariate linear, landmarks/outlines, function/performance
  • Reproducible workflows (R projects, R Markdown, Git/GitHub)

Consumables

  • Rolf (1990)
  • Klingenberg (2010)
  • Wainwright (2007)
  • In-class coding (1/14)

Deliverables:

  • AME1 release
  • Personal repo setup

Slides: Jan 13

Week 2 (Jan 19): Measurement, repeatability, and univariate characters

Topics

  • Trait definition, scaling, ratios, allometry
  • Scaling: size correction choices, ratio traps

Consumables

  • Symonds & Blomberg (2014)
  • Pelabon et al. (2014)

Deliverables:

  • AME1
  • CT1

Week 3 (Jan 26): Landmarks, Procrustes, and the geometry of form

Topics - Landmarks/semilandmarks; sliding - GPA, centroid size, tangent space - Deformation visualization (TPS grids, vectors) - geomorph ecosystem

Consumables

  • Martinez et al. (2013)
  • Mitteroecker & Gunz (2009)
  • Tiatragul et al. (2024)
  • Baken et al. (2021)

Deliverables:

  • AME3
  • CT3 (given in class 1/29) CPK: assign groups with Rmd

Week 4 (Feb 2): Outlines + Fourier descriptors

Topics - When landmarks fail: outlines and complex boundaries - Elliptic Fourier descriptors; reconstruction + harmonics - Biological examples: teeth, fins, leaves, shells, jaws

Consumables - Tseng et al (2022) - Bonhomme et al (2014)

Deliverables:

  • AME4
  • CT4

Week 5 (Feb 9): From ordination to inference (multivariate thinking)

Topics - PCA as a tool - Effect sizes, resampling, cross-validation ideas - Trajectories and modularity

Consumables

  • Abdi & Williams (2010)
  • Satterfield et al (2023)

Deliverables:

  • AME5
  • CT5

Week 6 (Feb 16): Comparative methods I

Topics

  • Models of evolution: Tempo and mode
  • PGLS and what it is (and is not)
  • Multivariate PGLS / phylogenetic MANOVA
  • Tree-linked visualization (evolutionary patterns)

Consumables

  • Zhusti et al.
  • Felsenstein (1985)
  • Symonds & Blomberg (2014)
  • Harmon (2019) Chapter 3 and Chapter 6

Deliverables:

  • AME6
  • CT6

Week 7 (Feb 23): Comparative methods II

Topics - BM vs OU intuition for shape data - Rate heterogeneity + regime shifts (claims you can/can’t make) - Disparity through time; clade comparisons

Consumables

  • Felice et al. (2021)
  • Uyeda et al (2018)

Deliverables:

  • AME7
  • CT7

Spring Break (Mar 2)

Week 8 (Mar 9): Evolutionary models of shape (BM, OU, rates, disparity)

Topics

  • Model comparison and hypothesis testing
  • Modularity and integration

Consumables

  • Evans et al. (2023)
  • Burns and Sidlauskas (2019)

Deliverables:

  • AME8
  • CT8

Deliverable:

Deliverable:
